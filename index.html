Diabetes Measurement Standards by Region

Standards vary primarily by continent and country. Expand each section for details.

HbA1c: DCCT % (<5.7% normal; ≥6.5% diabetes) with IFCC (mmol/mol) (<39; ≥48).

Blood Glucose:

Blood Glucose:
  • Brazil, Mexico: mg/dL (fasting: 70–99; ≥126)

HbA1c: IFCC rising; DCCT % still used.

Key Notes

  • HbA1c Conversion: IFCC (mmol/mol) = 10.93 × DCCT (%) − 23.5 (e.g., 6.5% ≈ 48 mmol/mol).
  • Regional Variations: Labs may dual-report units (mg/dL & mmol/L; % & mmol/mol).
  • Diagnostic Criteria: Fasting glucose ≥7.0 mmol/L or ≥126 mg/dL indicates diabetes.
  • Always consult local guidelines; exceptions exist.
